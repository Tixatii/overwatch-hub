The Heroes of Tomorrow

Each Hero in Overwatch is unique with their own weapons and abilities, but they generally fit into one of three classes: Tank, Damage (DPS), and Support. The standard team composition for 5v5 modes are 1 Tank, 2 DPS, 2 Support. 6v6 modes are similar, but have 2 Tank heroes instead.

Tank

Tank heroes are the core of an Overwatch team. Their primary role is to absorb damage and create space for their team. They achieve this goal by having a large health pool and by using defensive abilities such as shields or damage-reduction. Tanks are typically aggressive, but not too damaging, and require the player to have a lot of knowledge on the fundamentals of Overwatch’s gameplay to do their job effectively.

Damage

Damage - or DPS (Damage Per Second) - heroes are the typical “shooter” characters, that feel most similar to other games such as Call of Duty. Their primary job is to output damage and eliminate members of the opposing team so that their allies can secure objectives uncontested. This is also the largest and most diverse category, featuring over double the number of heroes than the other two categories. The roster contains heroes with varying ways of dealing damage, from long range snipers to up-close duellists and many in between.

Support

Support heroes do not take damage for their allies, nor is their primary focus to eliminate their opponents. The primary job of a Support hero is to heal their team and keep them alive, by restoring their allies’ HP whenever they take damage. Another key role of Support heroes is focused around providing utility to their team, such as increasing their speed or boosting their damage output, alongside cleansing negative effects such as burning or being immobilised.
