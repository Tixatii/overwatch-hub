Maps & Modes

Global Battlegrounds

Maps in Overwatch are tailor-made to specific game modes, and can only ever be played in those modes. They are also somewhat unique compared to other video games, as they are based on actual real-world locations on Earth - such as Rialto, a map set in Venice, Italy. The different game modes are explained below.

Escort & Hybrid

In the game mode Escort, and on their respective maps, teams take turns in two roles - the Attacking team and the Defending team. The attacking team must escort a moving payload objective to a delivery point (the end of the map), whilst the defending team tries to stop them until time runs out. There are checkpoints along the way that add time to the clock if they are reached. Hybrid is functionally the same, except the Attacking team must first capture a static point first, before the payload arrives and begins to move. An iconic Hybrid map, and one of the community’s favourites is King’s Row - set in London, England.

Control

In the Control game mode, teams fight over a centralised objective point, king-of-the-hill style, in a best-of-three format. When one team has uncontested control over the “capture point”, they will gain “capture percentage” up to a maximum of 100%, and the team that reaches 100% first will win that round. The first team to win two rounds, wins the match. One example of a Control map is Lijiang Tower, a futuristic metropolis set in China.

Push & Flashpoint

New game modes released with Overwatch 2 in 2022, these maps feature more dynamic gameplay than the older modes. Push features a neutral robot objective that can move in two directions - either towards the Blue team’s side of the map or towards the red team’s side - and will bring victory to whichever team pushes it to the opposite side of the map first, or whoever has pushed it the furthest overall by the end of the timer. Flashpoint is similar to control, where teams fight over contestable king-of-the-hill style points. However the format is best-of-five, the points are much quicker to capture, and the entire match is played on the same large-scale map. Two example maps in this mode are Colosseo (Push), set in Rome, Italy, and Suravasa (Flashpoint), set in India.
