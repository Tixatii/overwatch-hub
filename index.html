What is Overwatch?

Overwatch is a free-to-play, team-based, player-versus-player hero shooter created by Blizzard Entertainment, set in an alternate-timeline future Earth sometime in the mid-2070s. Players compete in teams of five or six (depending on the game mode), with each player piloting a character with unique weapons and abilities in an attempt to complete objectives and defeat the opposing team. The game features a host of different playable heroes, maps, and game modes, and is available on PC, PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch.

Lore and Legacy

The game's lore is vast, with supplementary materials such as cinematic shorts and comics that tell the story of a world in ruin following a war between humans and robots known as "Omnics". Overwatch has inspired a host of real-world merchandise and a thriving esports scene, and is to date one of the most-played competitive games of all time.

History

Overwatch was first released on 24 May 2016, and was permanently shut down on 3 October 2022. A sequel dubbed "Overwatch 2" was released at the same time, and the game became free-to-play. However, it is a common consensus amongst players and critics that the game had not changed much, save for some minor gameplay tweaks and new characters being added. In mid-2026, Blizzard removed the "2" from the game's title, naming it just "Overwatch" once again.
